Hardware

Smart Field Device

Purpose-built IoT hardware designed for Nepal's field conditions. Collect environmental data at the source, process it locally, and transmit to the cloud.

ESP32-S3 Powered

Solar-powered field unit with 4 integrated sensors — deployed in under 10 minutes

ESP32-S3 Microcontroller

Dual-core processor with built-in WiFi and Bluetooth. Handles sensor data acquisition, local processing, and secure cloud transmission with ultra-low power consumption.

Soil Moisture Sensor

Capacitive sensor measures volumetric water content in soil. Triggers irrigation alerts when moisture drops below optimal thresholds for your specific crop type.

Temperature & Humidity

DHT22 sensor provides accurate ambient temperature and relative humidity readings. Essential for predicting frost risk, heat stress, and optimal planting windows.

MQ Gas Sensor

Detects methane, ammonia, and other gases relevant to soil health and decomposition. Alerts on abnormal gas levels that may indicate crop disease or contamination.

Built for Nepal

Designed for Nepal's Agricultural Reality

Generic IoT solutions ignore the conditions that define Nepali farming. AgroPan is purpose-built for monsoon dependency, terrain variation, and smallholder economics.

66% of Nepal's population depends on agriculture for their livelihood

Hills vs. Terai Dynamics

Crop viability changes dramatically between the flat Terai plains and terraced mid-hill farms. AgroPan models both ecological zones with calibrated parameters — because what works in Chitwan fails in Sindhupalchok.

Monsoon Dependency

With 80%+ of agriculture rain-fed, a delayed monsoon can mean disaster. AgroPan factors in historical monsoon patterns and onset variability so farmers see timing-based risk before committing to a planting window.

Small Landholder Reality

The average Nepali farm is under 0.7 hectares. Every decision carries disproportionate risk. AgroPan's simulations are tuned for smallholder economics where margins are razor-thin and one bad season can mean food insecurity.

Market Price Volatility

Local mandi prices swing 30–50% within a single season. AgroPan integrates historical price data to project profit ranges, not just yield — because a bumper crop at crashed prices helps no one.

Closing the Data Gap

Nepal's agricultural data is fragmented across government offices, NGOs, and research stations. AgroPan consolidates 10+ years of climate, soil, and market records into a single simulation layer.

Policy-Grade Insights

Beyond individual farmers, AgroPan's simulation data can inform district-level agricultural planning, subsidy targeting, and climate adaptation strategies for Nepal's policymakers.
